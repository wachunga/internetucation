A Scottish inventor by the name of Robert Davidson designed an electric locomotive in the early 1800's. The cost of electricity, however, made its use impractical. Halfway through the 19th century the invention of an electric generator changed all this by lowering the cost of such current enough to make an electric railway possible. Germany would receive the first commercial electric street railway in 1881; America, the second in 1887. By the early 1900's, both Europe and America had numerous electric railways in operation.


The first generator-powered electric train

Eventually, the electric generator allowed the development of such high-speed passenger trains as the Shinkansen in Japan and the TGV in France.
