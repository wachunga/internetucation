Internetucation began as a single lesson designed specifically for a class at Chipman Forest Avenue School in New Brunswick, Canada. As this project's potential grew more apparent, however, plans were made to expand the site into an on-line collection of educational lessons: a medium of education in itself. Teachers now have access to a variety of pre-made lessons intended to take full advantage of the presentational advantages of today's modern technology. Teachers worldwide have access to the site, and can even suggest topics about which they would like to see a lesson created.

Unfortunately, with this aspiration comes an increase in required resources. It is for this reason that assistance is needed to allow the further development of Internetucation. If you have anything which you are willing to contribute, whatever it may be, please do so. The following is a list of types of support currently in demand:

  • Suggestions. If you feel that you have some good ideas related to the presentation of a certain topic, please visit the suggestions page.
  • Typed lessons or even web lessons. Such submissions, after undergoing the necessary editing/enhancement, will be added to the list of available lessons. Submissions greatly increase the number of topics covered and are therefore in the best interest of everyone.
  • Typing, research, programming, editing, testing, and other related services.

If you are able to contribute in one of these respects and are interested in the development of this project, please fill out the following form. There is no obligation once submitting your name and if after volunteering you are suddenly unable to assist, that is perfectly acceptable.
