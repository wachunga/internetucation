The success of Stephenson's lines excited financiers and engineers alike. The profit evident, many companies were formed to construct other such railways.

London was home to the very first train station, Euston, which was completed in 1846. Several more followed later on in the mid-19th century, including King's Cross, and Victoria.


King's Cross station in London

As the number of engines in use increased in Britain, prices dropped. Other countries admired Britain's advanced form of transportation and began building their own railways. In this manner, the railway spread across the world, boosting economy, encouraging foreign trade, and changing forever the way in which people travelled.
