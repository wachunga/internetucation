The Industrial Revolution of the 18th and 19th centuries heralded enormous social, political and technological changes. Frequent inventions fuelled industrialization's spread as it radically transformed almost every aspect of life. In much the same way as the Industrial Revolution redefined society, the railway did the concept of transportation. Furthermore, it reflected the level of technology at the time because with most inventions came corresponding advances in transportation. In this manner, the development of the railway parallelled that of industrialization for the duration of the Revolution. It is for this reason that the train is a trademark of this period.
