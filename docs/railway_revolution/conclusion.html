The progress of the Industrial Revolution and the Railway Revolution was simultaneous. Almost every invention contributed not only towards industrialization, but also, if indirectly, towards the establishment of the railway. Advanced iron smelting techniques, sophisticated steam-powered locomotives, and complex electric generators were all important milestones in the development of trains, without which modern transportation could never have advanced this far. Though their usefulness was debated while still in primitive form, trains have revolutionized the industry of transportation altogether and allowed so many new possibilities that without them, the Industrial Revolution would not have been quite so revolutionary.
